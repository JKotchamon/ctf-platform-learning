Hints (progressive)

Hint 1 (gentle)

The text is made entirely of characters 0–9 and a–f. That usually means you should think in pairs.

Hint 2 (medium)

Once you turn those pairs into symbols, you’ll see a strange language of dots, dashes, and slashes. It’s older than telephones.

Hint 3 (strong)

After the beeps become letters, they still read a bit “tilted.” Give every letter the same small rotation; digits and symbols stay put, and the shift wraps around the ends (…Z rolls back to A). Doing the same twist twice cancels it.

Hint 4 (final)

Now you’ll hold a message that only uses uppercase letters A–Z and the digits 2–7. It’s a way computers often turn binary into text for easier copying.

Hint 5 (bonus)

Every layer builds on the last — don’t skip steps. If your output looks nonsense, retrace and try peeling one layer at a time.

If you get stuck, consider whether you’re decoding in the right order — onions don’t peel from the middle 🧅.
